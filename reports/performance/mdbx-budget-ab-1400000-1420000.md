# Coordinated MDBX Commit-Budget A/B: 1,400,000-1,420,000

Both variants started from the verified full-state height-1,400,000 database,
used durable `NoWriteMap` MDBX and sixteen write-intent readers, and replayed
the identical 20,000-block range. Both reopened with zero MPT failures and
matched the reference root at height `1,420,000`:
`0xd2e9243b6b8e027f76f8af867f0d37afb4dfcbb34f9986bcdc5cc1d68dcb24a0`.

| Budget | MDBX commits | Import time | Overall BPS | Transaction-bearing BPS | Finalization | Cursor writes |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 16,384 control | 4 | 104.80 s | 190.84 | 680.55 | 96.35 s | 5.50 s |
| 32,768 candidate | 2 | 119.40 s | 167.50 | 584.46 | 109.63 s | 7.92 s |

The larger transaction reduced commit count but made cursor traversal and
total durable finalization slower. It is rejected for the production default.
The bounded `16,384` policy remains the current choice, with the environment
override available for further hardware-specific experiments.
